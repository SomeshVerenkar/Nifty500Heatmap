🎓 New to Sector Heatmaps? Here's a Quick Guide

What is a sector heatmap?
A visual dashboard grouping stocks by their industry (sector). Colours show performance — green = gains, red = losses. The brighter the shade, the stronger the move.
What is YTD (Year-to-Date)?
The total return from 1 January 2026 to today. A YTD of +12% means if you invested ₹1,00,000 on Jan 1, it is now worth ₹1,12,000.
What does P/E Ratio mean?
Price-to-Earnings ratio tells you how much investors are willing to pay per ₹1 of a company's profit. Lower P/E ≈ cheaper valuation (relative), higher P/E ≈ growth expectations.
What is Dividend Yield?
Annual dividend as a % of the stock price. A 2% yield means you earn ₹2 for every ₹100 invested just from dividends, regardless of stock price movement.
What are GICS sectors?
The Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS) divides all companies into 11 sectors: Financials, IT, Energy, Consumer Staples, Consumer Discretionary, Healthcare, Industrials, Materials, Utilities, Real Estate, and Communication Services.
Can any sector really go "2×" in 6 months?
A broad sector index doubling in 6 months is extremely rare and unlikely. The "2× potential" analysis here highlights sectors with high relative outperformance probability — not a guaranteed doubling. Always do your own research and consult a SEBI-registered advisor before investing.
